# 3. CINEMATOGRAPHY

## 3.1 Camera Philosophy

> **"The camera is a family member. It doesn't observe from outsideâ€”it lives inside these moments."**

### Movement Principles

| Moment Type | Camera Approach |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Intimate/Emotional | Handheld, breathing with subjects |
| Journey/Travel | Steady movement, gliding, drone |
| Triumph/Celebration | Dynamic, energetic, multiple angles |
| Reflection/Memory | Static, contemplative, patient |
| Time Passage | Match cuts, visual rhymes |

## 3.2 Aspect Ratios

| Format | Ratio | Usage |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Hero Spots (90"/60")** | 2.39:1 (Cinemascope) | Theatrical, epic |
| **Standard Spots (60"/45")** | 2.39:1 or 1.85:1 | Cinematic |
| **Social Cutdowns** | 16:9, 9:16, 1:1 | Platform native |
| **Mini-Documentary** | 2.39:1 | Cinematic consistency |

## 3.3 Lens Language

| Lens | Emotional Effect | Use For |
|------|------------------|---------|
| **24mm** | Scope, environment, journey | Landscapes, driving, stadiums |
| **35mm** | Intimate wide, environmental portrait | Family scenes, preparations |
| **50mm** | Natural eye, honest | Conversations, connections |
| **85mm** | Intimate close-up, separation | Emotional moments, faces |
| **135-200mm** | Compression, isolation | Baseball action, crowd faces |
| **Macro** | Detail, texture, significance | Hands, objects, sweat drops |

### Anamorphic Characteristics

We embrace anamorphic lens qualities:
- Oval bokeh
- Horizontal flares (controlled, not excessive)
- Edge softness
- Breathing on focus pulls

## 3.4 Camera Movement Dictionary

| Movement | Meaning | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **PUSH IN** | Increasing intensity, revelation | Approaching emotional climax |
| **PULL BACK** | Context, isolation, scale | Revealing the vast stadium |
| **TRACK ALONGSIDE** | Journey, companionship | Following the car on the road |
| **CRANE UP** | Transcendence, hope | Ending scenes, dreams taking flight |
| **HANDHELD FLOAT** | Intimacy, presence | Family moments, preparation |
| **STATIC LOCK** | Gravity, significance | Key emotional beats |
| **DRONE AERIAL** | God's eye, journey scope | Dominican landscape, stadium scale |

## 4.3 Lighting Philosophy

> **"Light should feel like it comes from God, not from a gaffer."**

### Natural Light Priorities

| Priority | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **1st Choice** | Actual golden hour (dawn/dusk) |
| **2nd Choice** | Natural light shaped with bounce/neg |
| **3rd Choice** | Motivated practicals enhanced |
| **Avoid** | Obviously artificial, flat, or harsh |

### Lighting by Location

| Location | Light Quality | Key Characteristics |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Dominican Home Interior** | Warm practicals, window light | Shafts of light through shutters, amber bulbs |
| **Car Interior** | Dashboard glow, streaming sun | Moving light patterns, golden wash |
| **Country Road** | Backlit, rim light | Sun behind mountains, car silhouettes |
| **Dirt Baseball Field** | Harsh midday, no apology | Real, sweat-inducing, authentic |
| **Gas Station** | Mixed (sun + canopy shade) | Bright patches, cool shadows |
| **MLB Stadium** | Dramatic artificial | Blue-white floods, skin warmth preserved |

## 4.4 Color Grading Direction

### Overall Grade Characteristics

| Parameter | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Contrast** | Medium-low, lifted blacks slightly |
| **Saturation** | Natural+, not oversaturated |
| **Highlights** | Warm roll-off, preserved detail |
| **Shadows** | Slightly blue/teal, never crushed |
| **Skin Tones** | Protected, warm, healthy |
| **Greens** | Natural Caribbean, not pushed |

### Grade by Act

| Act | Grade Style |
|-----|-------------|
| **Childhood/Origin** | Warmest, most nostalgic, slight softness |
| **Journey/Growth** | Natural warm, clean |
| **Triumph/MLB** | Slightly cooler contrast, more "present" |
| **Emotional Payoff** | Return to warmth, glow |

**Why He's Our North Star:**
Emmanuel "Chivo" Lubezki is THE cinematographer behind Terrence Malick's visual poetry. When we say "Malick style," we're really describing Lubezki's work. Three consecutive Academy Awards (Gravity, Birdman, The Revenant) cement him as the defining cinematographer of this era.

**What We Take:**

| Technique | Application to Our Campaign |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Natural Light Obsession** | Chase golden hour relentlessly; never fake it |
| **Long Takes with Movement** | Let scenes breathe; camera as family member |
| **Handheld Intimacy** | Camera that floats with subjects, breathing with them |
| **Wide Lenses Close to Subjects** | Environmental portraits that show context |
| **Backlight / Rim Light** | Subjects glowing against Dominican landscapes |
| **Practical Light Sources** | Car dashboards, window light, candles |
| **Magic Hour as Character** | Dawn and dusk ARE the visual signature |

**His Philosophy (Apply to Every Shot):**
> *"I try to use available light as much as possible. I want the light to feel real, to feel like it comes from a source that exists in the world."*

**Signature Lubezki Moments to Recreate:**
